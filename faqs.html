SEXUAL HARASSMENT

Frequently Asked Question (FAQs)
?

Can sexual harassment be perpetrated by someone of the same sex?

Yes. The prohibitions on sexual harassment are gender neutral, meaning both the perpetrator and the victim can be of the same sex. Sexual harassment can occur when the perpetrator is a male or female regardless of whether the victim is a male or female.

?

Does the harassment always have to be perpetrated by a boss or other supervisor?

No. The harasser can be the victim’s supervisor, an agent of the employer, a supervisor in another area, a co-worker, or a non-employee, such as a vendor for the business.

?

Is sexual harassment ever a criminal matter?

The harasser’s actions may be a crime, depending on the state in which they occurred. If the sexual harassment consisted of a physical attack, criminal sexual conduct, stalking, threats or another crime, the harasser may face criminal penalties.

?

If I have reported sexual harassment to HR or my manager and nothing was done, what should I do next?

You should contact an attorney to discuss your specific situation and determine what your next step should be and what your legal options are.

?

If I'm being sexually harassed by someone and some of the harassment has happened away from the office, is the company still liable for that?

If the harassment is ongoing, the company is aware of the harassment and has not taken steps to remedy the situation, all the harassment at work and outside of work is relevant and the company may be liable.

?

When should I contact an attorney if I think that I'm being sexually harassed?

You should contact an attorney immediately if you think you are being harassed to discuss your situation and determine your options and legal rights.
